## 3. Hazard Analysis

### 3.1 Primary Hazards

| Hazard | Risk Level | Mitigation |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Propeller Strike** | HIGH | Propeller guards, safety zones |
| **Battery Fire/Explosion** | HIGH | LiPo safety protocols, fire suppression |
| **Loss of Control** | MEDIUM | Failsafe systems, emergency stop |
| **RF Interference** | MEDIUM | Frequency management, compliance testing |
| **Electrical Shock** | LOW | Proper handling, insulated tools |

### 3.2 Secondary Hazards

- Eye injury from debris or laser sensors
- Noise exposure during extended operations
- Slipping hazards from equipment placement
- Data security and privacy concerns

## 5. Operating Procedures

### 5.1 Pre-Flight Safety Checklist

**Environment:**
- [ ] Flight area clear of personnel (minimum 3m radius)
- [ ] No loose objects or debris
- [ ] Adequate lighting conditions
- [ ] Weather conditions acceptable (no rain, wind <15mph)
- [ ] Emergency exits identified and clear

**System Checks:**
- [ ] Battery voltage and capacity verified
- [ ] Propellers secure and undamaged
- [ ] All connections secure
- [ ] Emergency stop functionality tested
- [ ] Communication link established
- [ ] Backup systems operational

**Personnel:**
- [ ] Safety officer present and briefed
- [ ] All personnel wearing required PPE
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities assigned
- [ ] Emergency procedures reviewed

### 5.2 Flight Operations

**Startup Sequence:**
1. Arm emergency stop system
2. Power on drone systems in sequence
3. Verify all systems nominal
4. Announce "DRONE STARTING" loudly
5. Begin operations only after "ALL CLEAR" confirmation

**During Flight:**
- Maintain visual contact with drone at all times
- Monitor battery levels continuously
- Keep emergency stop within immediate reach
- Maintain situational awareness of all personnel
- Follow planned flight path only

**Shutdown Sequence:**
1. Land drone in designated area
2. Disarm motors immediately
3. Power down systems in reverse sequence
4. Announce "DRONE SECURE"
5. Perform post-flight inspection

### 5.3 Emergency Procedures

**EMERGENCY STOP:**
1. Activate emergency stop immediately
2. Clear the area of all personnel
3. Do not approach until motors stop completely
4. Assess situation and call for assistance if needed

**Battery Fire:**
1. Evacuate area immediately (minimum 10m)
2. Call emergency services (911)
3. Use Class D extinguisher if safe to do so
4. Never use water on LiPo fires
5. Ventilate area after fire is controlled

**Lost Communication:**
1. Drone will automatically activate failsafe
2. Monitor drone for autonomous landing
3. Do not approach until completely powered down
4. Document incident and investigate cause

## 6. Battery Safety Protocols

### 6.1 LiPo Battery Handling

**Storage:**
- Store at 3.7V per cell (storage voltage)
- Use fireproof LiPo storage containers
- Store in cool, dry location away from flammables
- Check battery condition monthly

**Charging:**
- Use only approved LiPo chargers
- Never leave charging batteries unattended
- Charge in fireproof container or LiPo bag
- Monitor temperature during charging
- Charge at 1C rate maximum

**Disposal:**
- Discharge to 0V before disposal
- Use salt water discharge method
- Take to authorized electronics recycling center
- Never dispose in regular trash

### 6.2 Battery Emergency Procedures

**Swelling/Damage:**
- Stop using immediately
- Place in fireproof container
- Monitor for temperature increase
- Prepare for safe disposal

**Fire/Smoke:**
- Evacuate immediately
- Call emergency services
- Use sand or Class D extinguisher
- Never use water

## 7. RF Safety and Compliance

### 7.1 Regulatory Compliance

**FCC Compliance (US):**
- Operating frequencies: 2.4GHz, 5.8GHz (ISM bands)
- Maximum power output: <1W EIRP
- Equipment authorization required for commercial use

**RF Exposure Limits:**
- Maintain minimum 20cm distance during operation
- No prolonged exposure to antennas during transmission
- Follow SAR limits for handheld controllers

### 7.2 Frequency Coordination

- Check for conflicting RF sources before operation
- Coordinate with other wireless equipment in area
- Monitor for interference during operation
- Maintain backup communication methods

## 8. Facility Safety Requirements

### 8.1 Indoor Flight Environment

**Space Requirements:**
- Minimum ceiling height: 4 meters
- Minimum room dimensions: 6m x 6m
- Clear flight zones marked on floor
- Emergency exits clearly marked and accessible

**Environmental Controls:**
- Adequate ventilation for battery operations
- Appropriate lighting (minimum 500 lux)
- Temperature control (20-25°C)
- Humidity control (<60% RH)

### 8.2 Security and Access Control

- Restricted access during flight operations
- Proper storage of equipment when not in use
- Data security measures for sensitive information
- Visitor safety briefing requirements

## 9. Incident Reporting

### 9.1 Reportable Incidents

- Any injury to personnel
- Property damage
- Near-miss events
- Equipment malfunctions
- Safety procedure violations

### 9.2 Reporting Process

1. Immediate medical attention if required
2. Secure the area and equipment
3. Notify safety officer within 1 hour
4. Complete incident report within 24 hours
5. Conduct investigation and implement corrective actions
