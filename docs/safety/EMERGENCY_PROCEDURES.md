## 1. Emergency Stop Procedures

### 1.1 Manual Emergency Stop
**When to use:** Loss of control, unexpected behavior, imminent collision

**Actions:**
1. **IMMEDIATELY** press emergency stop button/transmit kill signal
2. **SHOUT "EMERGENCY STOP"** to alert all personnel
3. **CLEAR THE AREA** - minimum 5 meter radius
4. **DO NOT APPROACH** until all rotation stops
5. **ASSESS** situation and determine next steps
6. **DOCUMENT** incident in safety log

### 1.2 Automatic Emergency Stop Triggers
- Communication loss (>3 seconds)
- Battery voltage below critical threshold
- Excessive tilt angle (>45 degrees)
- Accelerometer impact detection
- Manual activation via remote or software

### 1.3 Post-Emergency Stop Actions
1. **Wait 30 seconds** before approaching
2. **Visually confirm** all motors stopped
3. **Disconnect battery** if safe to do so
4. **Inspect for damage** before handling
5. **Complete incident report**
6. **Do not restart** without safety officer approval

## 2. Battery Emergency Procedures

### 2.1 LiPo Battery Fire/Thermal Runaway

**WARNING SIGNS:**
- Swelling or puffing
- Excessive heat
- Smoke or unusual odor
- Hissing sounds
- Visible flames

**IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:**
1. **EVACUATE** area immediately (minimum 10 meters)
2. **CALL 911** - report chemical fire
3. **ALERT** others in building
4. **DO NOT USE WATER** - will make fire worse
5. **Use Class D extinguisher** only if safe to do so
6. **Ventilate area** after fire controlled

**NEVER:**
- Use water or standard fire extinguisher
- Attempt to move burning battery
- Inhale smoke or fumes
- Touch battery with bare hands

### 2.2 Battery Damage Assessment

**Safe Battery:**
- Normal temperature (room temp ±5°C)
- No swelling or deformation
- No unusual odors
- Voltage within normal range

**Damaged Battery - DO NOT USE:**
- Any swelling or puffing
- Cracks or punctures
- Excessive heat
- Voltage irregularities
- Previous impact damage

**Disposal Procedure for Damaged Batteries:**
1. Place in fireproof container with sand
2. Store in safe location away from flammables
3. Contact hazardous waste disposal service
4. Never dispose in regular trash

## 3. Communication Loss Procedures

### 3.1 Automatic Failsafe Sequence
When communication is lost, drone will:
1. **HOVER** in place for 5 seconds
2. **ATTEMPT** to re-establish communication
3. **INITIATE** return-to-home if no communication
4. **LAND** automatically in designated safe zone
5. **DISARM** motors after landing

### 3.2 Manual Recovery Actions
1. **DO NOT PANIC** - failsafe is designed to handle this
2. **MONITOR** drone behavior visually
3. **CLEAR** anticipated landing area
4. **ATTEMPT** to re-establish communication
5. **PREPARE** for emergency landing if needed
6. **WAIT** for complete shutdown before approaching

### 3.3 Communication System Backup
- Primary: Wi-Fi 2.4GHz
- Secondary: Wi-Fi 5GHz
- Emergency: Manual emergency stop transmitter
- Last resort: Physical intervention (if safe)

## 4. Medical Emergency Procedures

### 4.1 Injury Assessment
**MINOR INJURIES (cuts, bruises):**
- Apply first aid
- Clean and bandage wounds
- Document in incident log
- Monitor for complications

**SERIOUS INJURIES (bleeding, unconscious, suspected fractures):**
1. **CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY**
2. **DO NOT MOVE** injured person unless in immediate danger
3. **Apply pressure** to bleeding wounds
4. **Keep person** warm and comfortable
5. **Stay with person** until help arrives

### 4.2 Electrical Shock
1. **DO NOT TOUCH** the person directly
2. **TURN OFF** power source if possible
3. **Use wooden object** to separate from electrical source
4. **CALL 911** immediately
5. **Begin CPR** if trained and necessary
6. **Treat for shock** while waiting for help

### 4.3 Eye Injury
1. **DO NOT RUB** or touch the eye
2. **FLUSH** with clean water for 15 minutes
3. **COVER** both eyes with sterile gauge
4. **SEEK MEDICAL ATTENTION** immediately
5. **DO NOT** remove embedded objects

## 5. System Malfunction Procedures

### 5.1 Software/Control System Failure
**Symptoms:**
- Unresponsive controls
- Erratic behavior
- System freezes
- Error messages

**Actions:**
1. **ATTEMPT** emergency stop via software
2. **USE** hardware emergency stop if software fails
3. **MONITOR** drone behavior for automatic failsafe
4. **CLEAR** area and prepare for emergency landing
5. **DOCUMENT** all error messages and behavior

### 5.2 Sensor Failure
**GPS/Localization Loss:**
- Drone switches to manual control mode
- Maintain visual contact
- Land as soon as safely possible
- Do not attempt autonomous operations

**IMU/Gyroscope Failure:**
- Expect unstable flight behavior
- Immediate emergency landing required
- Clear area of all personnel
- Use emergency stop if unstable

### 5.3 Motor/Propeller Failure
**Single Motor Failure:**
- Drone will attempt controlled descent
- Clear landing area immediately
- Monitor descent path
- Prepare for hard landing

**Multiple Motor Failure:**
- Uncontrolled descent likely
- EVACUATE area immediately
- Call out "FALLING DRONE"
- Take cover behind solid barriers

## 6. Environmental Emergency Procedures

### 6.1 Weather-Related Emergencies
**High Winds:**
- Land immediately if winds >15 mph
- Secure all equipment
- Do not attempt flight in gusty conditions

**Lightning/Thunderstorms:**
- Cease all operations immediately
- Disconnect all electronic equipment
- Move to interior rooms
- Wait 30 minutes after last thunder

### 6.2 Power Outage
1. **LAND DRONE** immediately using backup power
2. **SECURE** all equipment
3. **USE** emergency lighting
4. **ACCOUNT** for all personnel
5. **WAIT** for power restoration before resuming

---

## 7. Evacuation Procedures

### 7.1 Building Evacuation
**Fire Alarm or Emergency:**
1. **EMERGENCY STOP** all drone operations
2. **SECURE** or abandon equipment as situation requires
3. **FOLLOW** building evacuation procedures
4. **ACCOUNT** for all team members at assembly point
5. **REPORT** to emergency personnel about drone equipment

### 7.2 Laboratory Evacuation
**Immediate Threats in Lab:**
1. **ACTIVATE** emergency stop
2. **SHUT DOWN** power if safe to do so
3. **EVACUATE** all personnel
4. **CLOSE** doors behind you
5. **CALL 911** from safe location

---

## 8. Post-Emergency Actions

### 8.1 Immediate Post-Emergency
1. **ENSURE** all personnel are safe and accounted for
2. **SECURE** the scene and equipment
3. **PROVIDE** medical attention as needed
4. **CONTACT** appropriate authorities
5. **DOCUMENT** everything while memory is fresh

### 8.2 Investigation and Reporting
1. **PRESERVE** evidence and equipment state
2. **INTERVIEW** all witnesses
3. **COMPLETE** detailed incident report
4. **ANALYZE** root causes
5. **IMPLEMENT** corrective actions
6. **UPDATE** procedures as needed

### 8.3 Return to Operations
**Requirements before resuming:**
- [ ] All personnel confirmed safe
- [ ] Equipment inspected and cleared
- [ ] Incident investigation completed
- [ ] Corrective actions implemented
- [ ] Safety officer approval obtained
- [ ] All team members briefed on lessons learned
